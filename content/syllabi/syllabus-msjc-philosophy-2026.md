Course Description

This course introduces philosophical ideas and methods concerning knowledge and the nature of reality. Using Plato's Republic as our primary guide, we will examine the nature of the soul, the definition of truth, the foundations of ethics, and the structure of a just society. We begin with the "big picture" of philosophical reflection before diving into one of Western Civilization's most influential primary sources.

Course Learning Outcomes (CLOs)

Identify and assess seeds of universal questions and themes in the history of philosophy within Plato.

Analyze and evaluate arguments and approaches to philosophical problems found in a primary texts.

Articulate and defend a stance on philosophical problems pertaining to reality, morality, and the self.

Course Schedule

Week 1: The Search for Wisdom (June 17 – June 21)

Module 1: The Philosophical Life

Reading: Sophie’s World (Ch. 1-3)

Focus: What is philosophy? The move from myth to reason.

Module 2: The Search for Truth

Reading: Republic Book I

Focus: Socratic dialogue and the chaotic world. Is "justice" just the interest of the stronger?

Holiday: June 19 (Juneteenth) - No assignments due.

Week 2: Justice and the Soul (June 22 – June 28)

Module 3: The Temptation of Injustice

Reading: Republic Book II (The Ring of Gyges)

Focus: Why be good if we can get away with being bad?

Module 4: The Power of Stories

Reading: Republic Book III

Focus: Censorship and entertainment. How do the stories we tell shape our souls?

Week 3: Harmony and the First Exam (June 29 – July 5)

Module 5: The Just State & Soul

Reading: Republic Book IV

Focus: Justice as "minding one's own business" and the tripartite soul.

MIDTERM EXAM: Covers Modules 1-5. Due Sunday, July 5.

Holiday: July 4 (Independence Day).

Week 4: Equality and the Good (July 6 – July 12)

Module 6: Society and Equality

Reading: Republic Book V

Focus: Can men and women be equal? The abolition of the private family.

Module 7: The Vision of the Good

Reading: Republic Book VI

Focus: The Sun, The Line, and the "Form of the Good."

Week 5: Reality and the Cave (July 13 – July 19)

Module 8: Escaping the Cave

Reading: Republic Book VII

Focus: Metaphysics and Epistemology. Are we living in a shadow-world?

Module 9: The Anatomy of Tyranny

Reading: Republic Book VIII & IX

Focus: Political devolution. Why the tyrant is the most miserable of men.

Week 6: Final Account (July 20 – July 25)

Reflections and Review

Reading: Republic Book X (The Myth of Er)

Focus: The immortality of the soul and the necessity of choice.

FINAL EXAM: Covers Modules 6-9 (plus cumulative themes). Due Saturday, July 25.
