YOU NEED QUESTIONS ON THE REPUBLIC? 

What, you are teaching the Republic and have great questions for Books I-IV but don't have good questions after that? 
Both reading comprehension and discussion questions, as well as essay prompts? I got you!


--- 
This study guide is organized by book and section to help you navigate the rich philosophical arguments of Plato's *Republic*.

Book V
------

-   **Four Forms of Badness:** What are the "four forms of badness"? Why is Glaucon interested in them? Is it wise to study badness?

-   **The Missing Section (449c):** Polemarchus and Adeimantus think Socrates is "robbing" them of a "whole section of the argument"---namely, that "women and children" will "be in common."

    -   Why does Socrates skip it?

    -   Why do they want him to go through it?

-   **Nature of the Sexes (451c-453c):** Are men and women different in nature?

-   **The Swimming Image (453d):** In the swimming in the pool or sea image, what is the "swimming" Socrates is talking about? What is the ocean? What might the dolphin be?

-   **Eristic vs. Dialectic (454a):** What is the difference between these two modes of discourse?

-   **Marriage Arrangements (459e):** What is the benefit of arranging marriages versus freely chosen marriages? What is the cost of each?

-   **The Greatest Evil (462b):** What does Socrates mean that "the [greatest] evil for a city ... splits it and makes it many instead of one..."? How does the argument propose to absolve factions? (See also 465c and 473b)

-   **Private Ownership (464c):** Is private ownership of land, children, and houses an evil, a good, or neutral? What is the benefit of communal ownership, according to Socrates?

-   **Possibility (466d):** The life of women and children in common has been explained. Is it possible? (See also 471c)

-   **The Three Waves (473c):** What are the three waves? Why is the third the "biggest"?

-   **The Many (474a):** Who are "the many" men who will attack Socrates "if he doesn't defend himself with speech and get away"? Why are they so mad?

-   **The Philosopher's Desire (475b):** Socrates and Glaucon assert, "that the philosopher is a desirer of wisdom, not of one part and not another, but of all of it." Is this true? If you don't like any kind of wisdom (botanical, astronomical, theological, physical, psychological), does that mean you are not a desirer of wisdom?

-   **Opinion vs. Knowledge (477a):** What is opinion? What is knowledge? What is the object of each?

Book VI
-------

-   **The Sharp-Sighted Guardian (484a):** What does it mean to be sharp-sighted? Who is sharp-sighted in this text?

-   **Swearing (484d):** Glaucon says, "By Zeus"... What do these swearing moments point to?

-   **Philosophic Nature (485b):** What makes a "philosophic nature," according to their argument? Do you agree?

-   **Memory (487a):** What does being "a good rememberer" have to do with the philosophic nature?

-   **Inexperience (487b):** What is wrong with those whom Adeimantus criticizes in this section, the "inexperienced at questioning and answering"?

-   **Images (487e):** Adeimantus says Socrates is "unaccustomed to speaking through images." Why is this so?

-   **The Shipowner (489a-b):** What is the meaning of Socrates' shipowner image? How does it "resemble the cities in their disposition toward the true philosopher"?

-   **Useless vs. Vicious (490e):** What's the difference between being "useless" and being "vicious"?

-   **Realistic Education (491-492):** Is the "course of learning" set down for the philosopher realistic? Does it matter?

-   **Worthy Philosophy (496b):** What does it mean to "keep company with philosophy in a way that's worthy"?

-   **Youthful Education (497):** What is so "eager and reckless" about Socrates' proposal for educating youth?

-   **Socrates and Thrasymachus (498b):** Were they enemies before? Why does Socrates say they weren't, and that they have become friends?

-   **The Outsiders (500):** Socrates defends "the many" from their hatred of philosophers by saying that they actually hate the "outsiders." Was Socrates hated in this way by the many? Could he "make the city happy"? (500e)

-   **The Digression (502d):** Socrates says it wasn't "very wise of me to have left aside" the digression about women, children, and rulers. Why did he change his mind? Which is right?

-   **The Guardian Philosopher (503b):** "Philosophers must be the most precise guardians." Describe his youth, education, military career, and the way he rules the city.

-   **The Finest Study (504e):** What is "the finest" study? What is "that for the sake of which it does everything"?

-   **Pleasure and Prudence (505b):** What is better, Pleasure, Prudence, or something else?

-   **The Good Itself (507b):** How does the idea of the good itself come into the conversation?

-   **Light, Sun, and Eye (507):** What is the point of the image about light, the sun, and the eye? How does that connect to the good?

-   **The Crown of Education (508):** How is the study of the idea of the good the crown of the education of the guardian/philosopher?

-   **Beyond Being (509a):** Socrates says "the good isn't being but is still beyond being, exceeding it in dignity and power." What does that mean?

-   **The Intelligible and Visible (509d):** What ratio exists between them? What are the four types of thinking that correspond to the four objects of thought?

-   **Hypothesis (511a):** How are "hypotheses" "the beginning of arts"?

-   **Glaucon's Soul (511d):** How does Glaucon understand and agree with Socrates? What's going on inside his soul at that moment?

Book VII
--------

-   **The Cave (514b):** Draw an image of the prison, a "cave-like" dwelling. Include as many details as possible, as if you had to build a set for a movie.

-   **The Release (515c):** Socrates says, "Take a man who is released and suddenly compelled to stand up." Who released him?

-   **Blindness (517):** How do the two causes of blindness relate to the argument?

-   **The Duty to Rule (520):** Why do philosophers have to rule? Is that unjust, as Glaucon suspects?

-   **Bigness and Littleness (523d):** How does the sight of bigness and littleness awaken the intellect?

-   **At a Loss (524):** Why is it "necessary... that... the soul be at a loss"? How does this awaken the intellect?

-   **Geometry (526):** How does geometry assist the pursuit of knowledge/wisdom?

-   **Astronomy (529):** How does astronomy assist the pursuit of knowledge/wisdom?

-   **Giving an Account (531):** Socrates' standard is that "men [be] able to give an account and receive one." Does anyone meet this standard?

-   **Dialectic (532):** What is the journey he calls dialectic? Relate the journey both within the image and without.

-   **Separating the Good (534a):** How do you separate out the idea of the good? Can you do it?

Book VIII & IX
--------------

-   **The Four Forms (546):** What are the four forms of regime and the four forms of men associated with them? Whence do they arise?

-   **Class and Soul (546):** Is Socrates classist? What makes someone a part of a specific class of soul?

-   **Soul Characteristics (550):** What are the characteristics of each soul that form that regime? How do these relate to Montesquieu's division?

-   **Oligarchic Pleasure (559):** What is the oligarchic man's orientation towards pleasure?

-   **Democratic Transition (559d):** How do the democratic men come from oligarchic ones?

-   **Tyranny (564a):** How does tyranny come out of democracy?

-   **The Tyrannical Man (571a):** Who is the tyrannical man? What is he like? Is anyone in the dialogue a tyrannical man?

-   **Desires (571b & 580d):** What are the forms of desires?

-   **Swearing (574b):** Again, Zeus---twice. Why are they swearing thus?

-   **Public vs. Private Life (578b):** Why doesn't the tyrannical man prefer the public life to the private life?

-   **Threefold Pleasure (580d):** What is the threefold division of pleasures? How do these point to parts of the soul?

-   **Wax vs. Words (588):** Is wax more moldable than words, or vice versa? Why?

Book X
------

-   **Imitative Poetry (595a):** Which kinds of poetry are excluded from the just city? Why shouldn't imitative poetry be admitted?

-   **Couch Analogy (597a):** What is the point of the "couch" analogy? How does this advance the argument?

-   **Ion Comparison (599):** Compare 599 with *Ion*---is Socrates implying the same thesis as the one Ion agrees to, or something different?

-   **The Goal of the Dialogue (600):** Why finish the dialogue---about justice, the good city, the philosopher-king, and the tyrannical soul---with a conversation about imitation?

-   **Deliberation and Emotion (605b):** Is the "deliberation" spoken about here non-emotional? Is it Christian?

-   **Personal Sorrow (605d):** What is the danger with personal sorrow? How does it fit into the good life? Is it okay to watch sad TV shows and movies?

-   **The Ancient Quarrel (607b):** Socrates describes an "old quarrel between philosophy and poetry." What compromise does he offer in 607c?

-   **The Final Myth (621a):** Why does the same man who prohibits imitative poetry end his conversation with a myth?

-   **Harm to the Soul (609b):** Can the soul be destroyed? Can it be harmed? Is justice more beneficial than injustice?

-   **Er's Choice (614):** Does Er make a good decision?
